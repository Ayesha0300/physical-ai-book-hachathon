## Exercise 2.1: Physics Engine Concepts
**Objective**: Explain the fundamental concepts of physics engines

**Instructions**: Write a 250-word explanation of what a physics engine does, including its role in simulation, the main components it handles, and why it's essential for robotics simulation. Include specific examples of physics phenomena that must be simulated for humanoid robots.

**Difficulty**: Beginner

**Expected Outcome**: A clear explanation of physics engine functionality with relevant examples for robotics.

## Exercise 2.2: Time Steps and Stability Analysis
**Objective**: Analyze the relationship between time steps and simulation stability

**Instructions**: Research and explain the relationship between time step size and simulation stability. Discuss:
- What happens with very large time steps
- What happens with very small time steps
- How to choose an appropriate time step for a humanoid robot simulation
- The tradeoffs between accuracy and performance

**Difficulty**: Intermediate

**Expected Outcome**: A comprehensive analysis of time step considerations in physics simulation.

## Exercise 2.3: Forces and Torque in Robotics

**Difficulty**: Intermediate

**Expected Outcome**: A detailed scenario with clear explanation of physics concepts applied to robotics.

## Exercise 2.4: Performance vs Realism Tradeoffs
**Objective**: Evaluate tradeoffs between simulation realism and performance

**Instructions**: Design a comparison between two different approaches to physics simulation for a humanoid robot: one optimized for realism and one optimized for performance. Discuss the specific differences in how each approach handles collisions, joint constraints, and environmental interactions. Analyze when you would choose each approach.
